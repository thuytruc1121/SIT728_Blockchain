Food Supply Chain DApp

Back to App

The Food Supply Chain DApp delivers transparent, real-time provenance for agricultural produce as it moves from farms to stores. Each batch is registered on a blockchain ledger, transferred only by authorised parties, verified for quality, and finally marked Sold by the store. Immutable records and role–based controls reduce fraud, improve recall precision, and build consumer trust. This white paper is written for managers, operators and auditors and outlines the business problem, the implemented solution, the user journey, and day-to-day use of the system.

The Problem We Solve

Food supply chains are multi-party and data are often fragmented across spreadsheets, PDFs and third-party systems. Two risks dominate:

  1. Duplication and inconsistency of batch information. The same goods may be recorded under different identifiers at different warehouses, leading to accidental mismatches or deliberate substitution, and making recalls costly and slow.
  2. Authority misuse by intermediaries. Warehouses or brokers may perform actions beyond their remit (e.g., transferring goods or declaring them sale-ready) without proper oversight or traceability.

Both issues undermine auditability and consumer confidence. A decentralised ledger provides a single, authoritative record per batch and enforces permissions in code rather than relying on manual process discipline.

Our Approach

  • Blockchain ledger provides a single source of truth for every batch.
  • Role-based custody (Farm → Warehouse → Store): only the current holder can transfer or update status.
  • Quality integrity: condition can change only from OK to Bad; reversal is disallowed to prevent tampering.
  • Finality: only a Store can mark a batch Sold, and only if it is both the current holder and at the correct lifecycle stage.
  • Provenance proofs: optional QR/IPFS links attach certificates (lab reports, origin documents) to a batch without storing sensitive content on-chain.

Stakeholders and Benefits

Farms gain a trusted origin story and faster onboarding of buyers. Warehouses receive clear custody logs and simpler audits. Stores ensure only verified, “OK” goods are sold, reducing liability. Customers can scan a QR code to verify the full journey (origin, stages, timestamps).

How It Works (User Journey)

1. Create a Batch (Farm)

Enter Batch name, Origin, Batch size, Expiry date, and optional Certificate hash (QR/IPFS). Press Create; a new on-chain record is minted and the farm becomes the first holder.

2. Transfer to Warehouse

As the current holder, enter the warehouse address and press Transfer. The stage auto-updates to Warehoused for authorised warehouse addresses.

3. Transfer to Store

The warehouse transfers custody to a store address; the stage auto-updates to Store.

4. Quality Incident (if any)

If an issue arises, the current holder may mark the batch as Bad. This decision is permanent; the batch cannot be transferred or sold thereafter.

5. Mark as Sold (Store only)

When ready for sale, the store—as current holder at stage Store—presses Mark as Sold. The lifecycle ends and the status is logged permanently.

Governance and Roles

For the prototype, actors are statically authorised at deployment:

  • Warehouses: 0xB4F1A67fec31ff504Df0C64fE456B1F4f63165E0
  • Stores: 0x0Aea37631823f63bE96694ba14fE7e0C5bd61E4A, 0x745C9102C727ccf84159Cd5E193b32583BC371c5

Future versions may replace static lists with governance to add or remove roles dynamically.

Data Privacy and Compliance

On-chain data are minimal (name, origin, counts, timestamps). Sensitive documents remain off-chain and are referenced via QR/IPFS hashes under owner control. All state changes emit events, forming an immutable, regulator-friendly audit trail.

Using the App

Connect MetaMask to the private chain; create a batch; click a row to open the modal; transfer only if you are the holder; update condition only from OK to Bad; mark as sold only as a Store at stage Store. The interface mirrors contract rules by disabling buttons when actions would revert.

Risk Controls

Tamper-proof logs: actions are recorded immutably. Irreversible “Bad”: prevents contaminated goods from re-entering circulation. Separation of duties: farms cannot sell; warehouses cannot sell; stores can sell only at the correct stage. Time-bound checks: expiry visibility supports future automated policies.
